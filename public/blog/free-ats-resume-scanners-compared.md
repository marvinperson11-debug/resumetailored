## What an ATS Scanner Actually Does

Before comparing tools, it helps to understand what ATS scanning actually means. An Applicant Tracking System is software used by employers to process the flood of incoming job applications. When you apply online, the ATS parses your resume, extracts your text, and compares it against criteria set by the hiring manager — primarily keywords related to required skills, job titles, and qualifications.

A resume that matches well gets surfaced to recruiters. One that matches poorly gets buried. The match is largely keyword-driven, which is why resume optimization tools focus on identifying which terms from the job description appear in your resume.

However, there is a crucial distinction that most tools blur: **identifying missing keywords is not the same as adding them.** Telling you that "Python" and "cross-functional collaboration" are missing from your resume still leaves you with the task of rewriting your resume to include them naturally. That is where the tools in this comparison differ dramatically.

## Jobscan Free Tier

Jobscan's free tier gives you five resume scans per month. Each scan compares your resume against a job posting and produces a detailed keyword match report, broken down by hard skills, soft skills, job title matches, and formatting compliance. The depth of reporting is impressive — you can see exactly which terms appear in the job description, how frequently, and whether they appear in your resume.

The limitation is the ceiling. Five scans per month sounds like enough until you are actually job searching. If you are applying to five jobs per week — a modest pace for many active seekers — you will burn through your free scans in a single week and face an upgrade decision before you have properly evaluated the product.

More importantly, even when you get a scan, nothing happens to your resume. Jobscan generates a report; you still have to open your resume, read the suggestions, and rewrite everything manually. For someone who is a strong writer, this works. For most people, this is where momentum dies.

## Teal Free Tier

Teal is a broader job search platform that includes a resume builder, job tracker, and ATS scoring. The free tier does include ATS keyword matching, but accessing it requires building your resume inside Teal's proprietary editor first — you cannot simply paste your existing resume and get an instant scan.

This friction is significant. If you have a resume you have been refining for years, being required to rebuild it from scratch inside a new tool before seeing any value is a major ask. Many users never get past the setup phase.

The AI writing features in Teal — including any resume rewriting or bullet point suggestions — are gated behind the paid plan. On the free tier, the ATS score is available but the tools to act on it are not. This creates the same diagnosis-without-treatment problem as other free tiers, but with an additional onboarding cost.

## Resume Worded Free Tier

Resume Worded operates similarly to Jobscan's free tier: you upload your resume, paste a job description, and receive a match score with suggestions. The tool also includes a "Targeted Resume" feature that shows keyword gaps and a general resume review with line-by-line feedback.

The free tier caps you at five resume reviews and five targeted scans per month. Like Jobscan, the feedback is diagnostic — you see what needs to change, but the actual rewriting is still your job. The interface is clean and reasonably intuitive, making it one of the easier free tools to use.

Resume Worded has a LinkedIn profile review feature similar to Jobscan's, which is worth noting. If optimizing your LinkedIn presence is part of your job search strategy, both Resume Worded and Jobscan have free versions of this tool.

## Bottom Line: Score vs. Fixed

**If you want a detailed ATS score and keyword diagnostic**, and you are comfortable rewriting your own resume based on suggestions, Jobscan's free tier is the strongest option. Five scans per month, detailed reporting, and a LinkedIn optimizer. Resume Worded is a close second.
