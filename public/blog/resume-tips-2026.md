# 10 Resume Tips That Will Get You More Interviews in 2026

**Category:** Resume Writing | **Read time:** 7 min | **Published:** May 2026

**Source:** https://resumetailored.com/blog/resume-tips-2026

---

The job market in 2026 is more competitive than ever. Remote work expanded the talent pool globally, AI tools changed what skills employers value, and economic shifts have made hiring cycles longer. The resume strategies that worked five years ago simply don't cut it anymore.

Here are the 10 resume tips that are actually making a difference right now.

---

## 1. Tailor your resume to every single job

This is the most impactful thing you can do — and the thing most people skip. Recruiters can instantly tell when a resume is generic. More importantly, ATS systems filter out resumes that don't match the job's keywords. Use the exact language from each job posting and prioritize the skills they mention first.

## 2. Lead with a strong professional summary

Skip the outdated "Objective" statement. Instead, open with a 2–3 sentence summary that positions you for the exact role you're applying for. Include your title, years of experience, and 1–2 key strengths relevant to the role.

## 3. Quantify everything you possibly can

"Managed social media" is forgettable. "Grew Instagram following from 2,400 to 18,000 in 6 months with a $0 ad budget" is memorable. Add numbers, percentages, dollar amounts, and timeframes wherever you can. It makes your impact concrete and credible.

## 4. Keep it to one or two pages

Unless you have 15+ years of highly relevant experience, keep it to one page. Two pages are fine for senior roles. Three pages get skipped. Recruiters don't read — they scan. Give them less to scan.

## 5. Use bullet points, not paragraphs

No one reads a paragraph in a resume. Lead each bullet with a strong action verb: Led, Built, Drove, Increased, Reduced, Launched, Managed. Keep bullets to 1–2 lines. Start with the result, then explain how.

## 6. Include a dedicated skills section

Put a clean skills section near the top of your resume. Include both hard skills (specific tools, languages, platforms) and relevant soft skills. This makes it easy for ATS to find your qualifications and lets recruiters quickly confirm you're a fit.

## 7. Remove anything older than 10–15 years

Unless it's directly relevant, experience from 15+ years ago takes up space and dates you. Focus on your last 3–5 roles. If you have older experience that's truly relevant, keep just the title and company — no bullet points.

## 8. Use a clean, professional format

Fancy designs with graphics, icons, and two-column layouts look great but often break ATS parsers. Use clean formatting, readable fonts (Inter, Calibri, Georgia), and consistent spacing. Save the creative design for industries where it's expected, like graphic design.

## 9. Proofread — then proofread again

Spelling errors and grammatical mistakes are an automatic rejection for many recruiters. Use spell check, then read it aloud, then have someone else read it. A single typo on a resume suggests carelessness, and that's the last impression you want.

## 10. Add a LinkedIn URL — and make sure it matches

Recruiters will check your LinkedIn. Make sure your LinkedIn profile matches your resume in terms of job titles, dates, and accomplishments. Inconsistencies raise red flags. Add your custom LinkedIn URL to the top of your resume.

---

> **Quick win:** Print your resume and look at it from 3 feet away. What stands out? That's what the recruiter sees in the first 7 seconds. Make sure your name, most recent title, and top company are immediately clear.
