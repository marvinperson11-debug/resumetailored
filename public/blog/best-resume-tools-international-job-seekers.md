## The 4 Unique Challenges for International Applicants

### 1. Resume Format Varies Dramatically by Country

US resumes: 1 page, no photo, no date of birth  
German resumes (Lebenslauf): 2+ pages, photo expected, date of birth required  
Chinese resumes: Often structured with personal details that would be illegal to request in the US  
UK resumes (CVs): 2 pages standard, often includes references section

An AI tool trained on US resume data will produce US-format output even when you ask for a German or Chinese version. This is a critical gap.

### 2. Bilingual Content Requires More Than Translation

A Chinese engineer applying to US tech companies needs more than a translated resume. They need:
- US-standard action verbs (not direct translations of Chinese equivalents)
- Company names that may need both English and Chinese versions
- Degree equivalency language (Tsinghua University → equivalent framing for US recruiters)
- ATS-compatible formatting (some bilingual resumes break ATS parsing)

### 3. Visa Status Language Matters

How you communicate work authorization affects both ATS filtering (some job listings exclude non-authorized applicants at the software level) and recruiter perception. The right phrase at the right stage in the application matters.

### 4. Keyword Differences Across English-Speaking Markets

"Maths" vs "Math." "CV" vs "Resume." "Redundancy" vs "Layoff." British English usage in a US ATS system can cause unexpected keyword misses. The reverse is also true.

## What to Look for in a Tool

Before comparing tools, know the criteria that matter for international use:

- **Accepts non-English input** (Chinese, German, Spanish, etc.)
- **Produces output in target language** (not just translated)
- **Handles format switching** (US resume vs German Lebenslauf)
- **Understands international degree/institution naming**
- **Supports visa status disclosure language**
- **Doesn't require US address or phone format**
