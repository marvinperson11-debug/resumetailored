What every strong cover letter has in common

Whatever the role, the letters that get read share the same bones: a first line that proves you can do this job (not a throat-clearing "I am writing to apply"), one or two achievements with numbers that mirror the posting's priorities, a sentence on why this company specifically, and a confident close. Keep it to half a page. ResumeTailored builds all of that from your real resume and the actual job description — so it's specific, truthful, and fast.
