The Right Approach

What "tailoring" actually means

Most job seekers think tailoring means swapping a few keywords at the top of their resume. That's not how it works. ATS systems and recruiters both look for the job's specific language woven naturally throughout your experience — not bolted on.

Real tailoring means rewriting your bullet points so that your experience is described using the same words, tools, and outcomes the job description uses. A role asking for "cross-functional stakeholder alignment" needs to see that phrase — not "team collaboration."

Keyword stuffing gets your resume flagged. Natural rewriting gets you interviews.

No. The AI only rewrites and reframes your existing experience using the job's language. It never adds skills, roles, or achievements you don't have — you stay 100% truthful while communicating your real value more clearly to both ATS systems and human recruiters.

Real tailoring means rewriting your bullet points so the job's language appears naturally in context — showing how your experience maps to their specific requirements.
